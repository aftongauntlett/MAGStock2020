Rules

  1. We decide the camping spots. We’re trying to keep track of where people are to help people find each other. So, don’t move or spread into your neighbor’s area unless you get permission from both them and us.
  2. Pets are welcome, but we strictly apply the “no bark – no bite” policy, a leash policy and clean up after pet rules.
  3. The campground quiet hours are from 11 pm to 8 am. Please keep noise down during this time.
  4. We’re all crammed together pretty tight. Be considerate to others. Don’t beg, steal. If your neighbor asks you to keep it down, try to do so. Don’t harass others at the campground.
  5. Fire safety is a must. No unattended fires! Fires must be in a fire ring or pit. If there is not a fire ring or pit, find one! No setting random things on fire.
  6. No food? There are stores nearby. It’s OK to barter and share with others, but don’t expect/steal provisions. Do not sell food outright.
  7. Drinking (if you’re of age) is fine. Don’t drink to the point of belligerence or fall-down drunk. If you reach this point, we’ll ask you to go to your tent to sleep it off. Don’t puke on the moon bounce.
  8. No alcohol is permitted at or on the lake.
  9. Dispose of your trash. We’re not your parents! We don’t want to ask you to clean up after yourself.
  10. Stay hydrated! Heatstroke will not be noticed until it is too late. CONSTANTLY. DRINK. WATER.
  11. If you brought stuff to sell, don’t be pushy or shady about it. Contact us at admin@magstock.org so we can assess the situation and help you. Items that will not be acceptable for sale include, but are not limited to: alcohol, drugs, and fireworks.
  12. No attempting to drown people or items in the lake.
  13. No vandalizing the campground or anyone else’s belongings.
  14. Wear your wristband at all times.
  15. Checkout time is 12:00 PM Sunday – this is when your gear should be mostly packed and your campsite cleaned up. Feel free to stick around for longer to help out or get some last-minute hangs though!
  16. Children under 18 must be at their campsite and under a parent’s supervision after 11pm.
  17. The campground does not allow the use of any fireworks, please keep these at home.
  18. Firearms and other weapons are prohibited as stated in the MAGFest code of conduct.
  19. For your safety, do NOT enter the lake after dark.
  20. Large vehicles (including but not limited to U-Hauls, Penskes, RVs, etc.) are not permitted without prior written authorization from MAGStock.
  21. Large items (including but not limited to furnature, mattresses, storage structures, inflatables, etc.) may not be disposed of at the campground and must be removed by the owner before leaving the campground.
  22. Read and abide by the Small Country Campground Rules (available on-site).
  23. And lastly, read and abide the MAGFest Code of Conduct.

Camping Info

Haven’t been camping before? Need a checklist because you probably forgot something? Here are the basics:

“The Essentials”

  • A tent with enough room for everyone, or friends you trust with tents
  • Sleeping bag or something to keep you warm at night
  • Folding tables, chairs
  • Light-duty rope and/or twine
  • Cooler (and ice, available for purchase at the campground)
  • Food and drink (there are nearby stores, but you will be cooking stuff, or eating cold food)
  • Trash bags
  • Bug spray / bug repellent
  • Flashlights or lantern
  • Extra batteries
  • Clothes
  • Swimsuit
  • A towel
  • Extension cords
  • Sunscreen
  • Hand Sanitizer
  • Body Wash, Shampoo, Conditioner - stay clean people!

  • Portables (Switch, Zune, etc…)
  • Cell Phone

  • Extra money for goodies, pizza, emergencies, etc…
  • Plastic bins for washing dishes
  • Grill

FAQ

I want to make gobs of money, and sell things to unsuspecting campers!

  • We handle this on a case-by-case basis. Email us at admin@magstock.org and let us know what you are looking to do.

Quiet hours? What is this?

  • Local noise ordinances mean we cannot play amplified music after 11pm. Please do not play amplified music between the hours of 11pm and 8am./li>

How are we going to play games at a campground? Is there going to be any power?

  • The campground has more power than the Hilton Mark Center. No, seriously.You will need to get creative so your things do not get wet.

Are there showers? Running water?

  • Most definitely yes, and if you don’t take a shower we are throwing you in the lake. There are showers in the bath house in the middle of the Black camping area, as well as next to the Manor House.

There’s a lake?

  • Yes, there most certainly is a lake.

Is there WiFi?

  • It’s slow, but it’s there. You may want to use cell phone internet, but even that’s a little iffy. You should be able to at least check your email and facebook!

Can I bring my dog?

  • Pets are allowed and there is no charge. They must be kept on a leash at all times. The site has aggressive breed restrictions so please call them first. There’s a no barking and biting policy and a maximum of 3 pets allowed per campsite. Since we’re all going to be crammed together you may want to consider leaving your pet in the care of someone at home.

Can I bring my kids?

  • Kids are allowed and there is no charge. They must be kept on a leash at all times. The site has aggressive breed restrictions so please call them first. There’s a no barking and biting policy and a maximum of 18 kids allowed per campsite. Since we’re all going to be crammed together you may want to consider leaving your kid in the care of someone at home.

Where am I going to get food?

  • Louisa is nearby and has some food places and you should certainly bring snacks and drinks for yourself and friends! We also sell a limited number of food passes for the weekend that get you 3 meals/day cooked by our awesome staff.

Can I drink?

  • Alcohol consumption is allowed at the campground. You are not permitted to sell alcohol onsite. Enjoy responsibly! Keep in mind that it is a lot easier to get dehydrated while outdoors. All laws continue to apply. Alcohol is not permitted on the lake or the beach. If you leave a glass bottle on the beach we will find you.

WHAT IS THIS MOON BOUNCE YOU SPEAK OF?

  • They’ve got a moon bounce buried in the sand and it rules. Also a floating thing in the lake.

ARE YOU SERIOUS!?

  • YES WE ARE!


This sounds really awesome, but I have more questions. Ask us! admin@magstock.org
